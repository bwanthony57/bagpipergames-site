# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

- **Players and fans** looking for approachable indie tabletop games — browsing on phone or desktop, often arriving from a game site or social link.
- **Support contacts** for Golfer's Pair-A-Dice who need a clear path to email help.
- **Business partners and press** who want to understand BAGPIPER, LLC and reach the right inbox.

Context is informational, not transactional. Visitors skim, decide whether the studio feels trustworthy and fun, and click through to a game or send email.

## Product Purpose

Bagpiper Games is the publishing label for BAGPIPER, LLC — a small independent game company in Fort Worth, Texas. This site introduces the label, showcases current releases, and provides contact paths. Success looks like: visitors understand who we are, feel the brand personality (luck, strategy, clubhouse energy), and take the right next step (visit a game site or email us).

## Brand Personality

**Approachable · Replayable · Personality**

Warm and confident without corporate stiffness. Feels like a well-loved clubhouse — a little luck, a little strategy, a lot of character. Independent and thoughtful: we ship one game at a time, with care. Not loud or gimmicky; not sterile or "indie slop."

## Anti-references

- Generic AI landing pages: purple gradients, Inter-as-default with no intent, card grids with identical drop shadows.
- Over-designed game studio sites with cinematic video heroes and hollow buzzwords.
- Corporate SaaS aesthetics (gray sidebar dashboards, feature comparison tables).
- Introducing extra red/warm accents beyond the logo pennant — the pennant is the single warm accent in the identity system.
- Restyling or recoloring logo components; the mark is fixed per BRAND-SPEC.md.

## Design Principles

1. **Identity-preservation wins** — Logo, pennant accent, and deep forest greens are non-negotiable. Site UI extends the brand; it does not reinvent it.
2. **Clarity over cleverness** — One primary action per section. Support and business emails are easy to find.
3. **Warmth on dark** — Deep green backgrounds with cream text feel like evening on the course, not a dark-mode dashboard.
4. **Small studio honesty** — Copy and layout reflect that we are independent and deliberate, not a AAA publisher.
5. **Accessible by default** — Readable contrast, focus states, semantic HTML, and reduced-motion respect.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG 2.1 AA for text contrast and interactive elements.
- Semantic landmarks, heading hierarchy, and descriptive link text.
- Visible focus indicators on all interactive elements.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for any future animation.
- Muted body text must still meet 4.5:1 contrast against backgrounds.
